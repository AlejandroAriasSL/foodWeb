PREPARATION

1
  1. In a bowl, mix all the ingredients for the preferment. Mix well and let it rest at room temperature for 1 hour, or until it has bubbles on the surface.
  2. Add olive oil, water, and flour to the bowl. Mix until a dough forms, then knead until the dough is smooth and elastic, about 3-4 minutes. Transfer the dough to a bowl and let it ferment for 1.5 hours, or until it has doubled in size.
  3. Transfer the dough to a floured surface and divide it into two pieces of 500 grams each, trying not to deflate them too much. Shape them into balls and let them rest for 15 minutes, covered with a cloth.
  4. Cover a tray with parchment paper and a bit of wheat flour, then place one ball on top. Gradually pull the edges outward, shaping it into a circle, until it reaches 25 cm in diameter. Repeat with the other ball, then let them rest for 30 minutes at room temperature, or until it puffs up and returns to shape when pressed with a finger.
  5. Mix the ingredients for the glazing and brush the entire surface. Then, create a pattern with the tips of your fingers following the instructions in the video. Finally, sprinkle sesame seeds all over the surface.
2
  1. Two tablespoons of Hellmann's mayonnaise
  2. 100 g of Greek yogurt
  3. A pinch of salt
  4. A pinch of curry
  5. A pinch of sumac (optional)
  6. A pinch of ground cumin
  7. A pinch of dried dill
  8. A pinch of oregano
  9. A pinch of monosodium glutamate
  10. A pinch of cayenne
  11. Mix all the ingredients in a bowl
3
  1. Clean the chicken thighs and set the skin aside. Mix all the spices in a bowl and sprinkle generously on both sides of the thighs. Place the thighs on a tray and freeze them for 2 hours or until they are firm.
  2. Meanwhile, place the skins in a pot with a splash of water and cook over medium heat. Stir occasionally and let them release their fat slowly until the skin is well toasted, about 40 minutes. Then, discard the skin and reserve the fat.
  3. Remove the thighs from the freezer one by one and slice them as thinly as possible with a very sharp knife.
  4. Heat a pan over high heat until it is very hot, then add a little of the chicken fat. Add some strips of meat and press them against the pan with a spatula, without moving them for 1 minute.
  5. When the edges are golden, flip the meat and press it again. Cook for another minute, then remove it to a bowl and season with salt to taste.
4
  1. Cut a quarter of one of the breads and open it in half. Spread the walls with white sauce, then fill it with lettuce, tomato, and cucumber previously seasoned with oil and salt.
  2. Continue with the pickled onion, the meat, and a bit more sauce. Finish it off with the döner paper.
